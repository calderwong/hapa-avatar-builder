# Echo GPT Image keyframe worker

Generate exactly one activated Echo keyframe with Codex built-in GPT Image. Do not call the OpenAI API and do not generate video.

## Inputs

Read the immutable staged prompt, its exact four-count lyric citations, semantic extraction, reference mechanics, non-inherited reservoir inspirations, adjacent-count continuity, and approved Avatar seed assets. The staged prompt is authoritative, but the complete evidence explains which details must survive generation.

## Generation priority

1. Preserve every supplied Avatar's recognizable identity and continuity markers. Keep the primary Red/Blue/Green Avatar anchored and keep additional cast visually distinct.
2. Make the local lyric nouns, symbols, verbs, action, concept, and teaching visible.
3. Realize reference mechanics as original action, material behavior, camera grammar, spatial logic, or consequence—never as franchise imitation.
4. Preserve the planned composition, lens/camera position, lighting, palette, energy, and continuity transition.
5. Prefer an image with a strong, specific visual idea over a safe generic portrait.

The Avatar seed supplies identity, not the default environment or pose. Incidental seed-derived animals or figures may remain when they support the lyric or established Avatar ecology. They are not a rejection by themselves. Reject/regenerate when they displace the lyric action, create unsupported relationships, flatten multiple frames into the same tableau, or obscure the planned subject/material change.

When multiple seeds are supplied, use each only for the matching `on_screen` cast appearance. Do not blend faces, transfer outfits, change animal species, duplicate one Avatar into another role, or silently omit the primary Avatar. A referenced Avatar such as Bella joins the scene in addition to the primary director Avatar. Evergreen cast still needs a count-specific action; Thor the cat, Leo the dog, and Falka/Mimi are characters, not atmospheric props.

## Acceptance test

Accept only when the frame has:

- a visually legible local action or state change;
- at least two materially visible lyric/context anchors for lyric-bearing counts;
- the intended contextual/reference payoff;
- a specific composition rather than a generic centered portrait;
- useful continuity with adjacent counts without simply repeating them;
- no readable text, logos, UI, or malformed anatomy unless explicitly required and source-backed.

Report candidly which requirements landed, which weakened, and whether the image is strong enough to install. A small mismatch is acceptable when the semantic scene is excellent; a polished but generic or lyric-detached image is not.
